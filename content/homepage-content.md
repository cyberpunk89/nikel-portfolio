## About Section

**Section label:** About me

**Main bio:**

I'm Ninad — most people call me Nikel. I'm a UI/UX designer with 5+ years of experience working across B2B SaaS, fintech, and e-commerce.

I like working in the gap between research and execution — the place where all that messy insight about users finally becomes something tangible and useful. I've led end-to-end design as the sole designer on products, built design systems from nothing, run 20+ user interviews, and shipped work that moved real metrics.

Most recently at Scalar (ZF Group), I owned the full UX trajectory for an asset tracking module used by 200+ organisations — from discovery to delivery, working alongside 10 engineers and a product manager. Before that at Sirelo, I rebuilt their design system from scratch and led a full website redesign.

Outside of work I'm into D&D, illustration, coffee, PlayStation, cooking, and going down rabbit holes. I take my hobbies as seriously as my design briefs.

## Structural Notes for Dev / Design Reference

### Recommended Page Order
1. Hero (name, title, CTA)
2. Work (project grid — leads with the strongest content)
3. About (bio + timeline — comes after work so projects speak first)
4. Skills (supporting detail)
5. Writing (bonus content for people who want more)
6. Contact (close strong)

### Tone Reminders
- Warm and direct — not salesy, not overly casual
- Puns *only* in: hero easter egg, footer flavor text, and section intros (1 max per section)
- Everywhere else: clear, confident, grounded
- Retro pixel art aesthetic should live in the *visual* layer — copy doesn't need to do the heavy lifting there
- Numbers and results where they exist (50% visit increase, 200+ orgs, $400K funding) — these build credibility fast
